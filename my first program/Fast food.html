Definition: Fast food refers to food that is quickly prepared and served, emphasizing speed of service. Preparation: Many fast food items are pre-prepared or cooked in bulk, allowing for rapid service. Availability: It's often found at chain restaurants, take-out places, and other quick-service establishments. Common Items: Examples include burgers, fries, pizza, sandwiches, hot dogs, and chicken. Health Considerations: Fast food is often criticized for being high in calories, sodium, and unhealthy fats, and for being low in nutritional value.
