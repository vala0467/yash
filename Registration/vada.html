Vadas are a type of deep-fried savory snack commonly found in Indian cuisine, especially South India. They are made with various ingredients like lentils, vegetables, and spices. There are many different types of vadas, each with unique flavors and textures. Here are some popular types of vadas: Medu Vada: A South Indian fritter made with urad dal (black gram) flour, known for its crispy exterior and soft interior. Batata Vada: A popular street food from Maharashtra, featuring a spicy potato filling wrapped in a chickpea flour batter. Masala Vada: A disk-shaped vada made with toor dal (whole lentils) and various spices. Maddur Vada: A crispy snack from Karnataka, made with rice flour, semolina, onions, and spices. Dahi Vada: A North Indian snack where fried vadas are soaked in yogurt (curd) and seasoned with spices. Sabudana Vada: A popular snack made with tapioca pearls, mashed potatoes, and spices, often eaten during fasting. Vada Pav: A Maharashtrian street food consisting of a fried vada served inside a bread bun (pav). Mirchi Vada: A spicy snack made by stuffing green chilies with a potato and spice filling, then deep-fried. Moong Dal Vada: A popular snack made with moong dal (split green gram) and spices, known for its crunchy exterior and soft interior.
